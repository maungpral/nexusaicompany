Shares Tokenization and How It Can Transform Your Business

Sergey.O

27.09.2023

Share:

Share on FacebookShare on X (Twitter)

Tokenized stocks represent the next frontier in the financial landscape, revolutionizing the way businesses raise capital and investors access global markets. By leveraging the power of blockchain technology, tokenization enables fractional ownership of shares, fractional dividends, and seamless cross-border trading. 

The tokenization industry is experiencing rapid growth, with the global market predicted to reach $24 trillion by 2027. This adoption is driven by the increasing interest from traditional financial institutions, such as the New York Stock Exchange and Nasdaq, which are exploring ways to tokenize their assets.

In today’s article, we will explore the history of the concept of shares tokenization, its principles, real-life successful examples, and its benefits. Enjoy reading!

The Genesis of Tokenized Stocks

The concept of tokenized stocks appeared in the early 2010s with the emergence of Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. However, it wasn’t until 2017 that the first tokenized stock exchange, Overstock.com, launched. Since then, the tokenization industry has evolved rapidly, with new platforms and products constantly emerging.

Blockchain technology plays a vital role in the tokenization process. By leveraging its decentralized and immutable nature, blockchain enables the secure and transparent issuance and trading of tokenized stocks. The smart contracts (besides being the actual on-chain representation of assets) are used to automate various aspects of the tokenization process, such as dividend distribution and voting rights.

The Ricardian contracts concept is also worth mentioning to better understand the topic. Ricardian contracts are a type of smart contract that can be used to represent the terms and conditions of a legal agreement in a machine-readable format. This makes them ideal for use in tokenized stocks, as they can be used to automate the issuance and trading of even more complex securities and traditional assets. Therefore, Ricardian contracts can be used to create new types of financial products and services, such as tokenized funds and tokenized derivatives.

What Is a Tokenized Stock?

Tokenized stocks are digital representations of traditional shares, issued on a blockchain network. Tokenization involves creating a digital token for each share of stock. These tokens can then be traded on decentralized exchanges, offering investors unprecedented liquidity and accessibility.

To tokenize a stock, a company must first create a digital asset tokenized stock (DATS). This is done by creating a smart contract on a blockchain network like Ethereum. The smart contract will define the terms and conditions of the tokenized stock, such as the number of shares it represents, the voting rights it grants, and the dividends it pays.

Once the smart contract is created, the company can issue the DATS tokens to investors. Investors can trade the DATS tokens on decentralized exchanges or use them to participate in corporate governance activities, such as voting on shareholder proposals.

Tokenized stocks offer a number of benefits for both businesses and investors, including:

  • Fractional ownership: Tokenized stocks can be purchased in fractional units, making them accessible to investors with all levels of income.
  • Fractional dividends: Tokenized stocks holders receive fractional dividends, proportional to their ownership stake.
  • Seamless cross-border trading: Tokenized stocks can be traded on decentralized exchanges, which are accessible to investors from all over the world.
  • Reduced costs: Tokenized stocks can help businesses reduce the costs associated with raising capital and trading shares.
  • Increased liquidity: Tokenized stocks offer investors greater liquidity, as they can be traded 24/7 on decentralized exchanges and even can be used as a collateral or deposit in DeFi protocols.

Generally, one of the key advantages of tokenized stocks is that they can be used to create new types of financial products and services. For example, tokenized stocks can be used to create tokenized funds, which are investment vehicles that track the performance of a basket of stocks. Tokenized stocks can also be used to create tokenized derivatives, which are financial contracts that derive their value from the underlying price of a stock.

Tokenized stocks are a new and innovative asset class that offers a number of advantages over traditional stocks. As the technology continues to mature and regulatory clarity emerges, tokenized stocks are expected to become increasingly mainstream.

The Evolutionary Timeline

The story of tokenized stocks is one of constant evolution. These digital assets have undergone a remarkable journey from their inception to the present day. We’ll trace the history and progression, revealing the pivotal moments that have shaped their existence.

The tokenization industry has evolved rapidly over the past few years, with significant milestones achieved. Here is a more detailed overview of them:

  • 2013: Ethereum is launched, providing a platform for the development of dDpps and smart contracts.
  • 2016: Overstock.com announces plans to tokenize its shares, making it the first company to do so.
  • 2017: Overstock.com launches its tokenized stock exchange, tZERO.
  • 2018: tZERO partners with Nasdaq to launch a regulated security token trading platform.
  • 2019: Fidelity Investments launches a digital asset platform to support tokenized securities.
  • 2020: The New York Stock Exchange announces plans to tokenize its assets.
  • 2021: The first tokenized ETF, the ProShares Bitcoin Strategy ETF, launches on the New York Stock Exchange.

The tokenization industry is still in its early stages of development, but it has the potential to revolutionize the way businesses raise capital and investors access global markets. As technology matures and regulatory clarity emerges, tokenized stocks are expected to become increasingly mainstream.

The Blockchain Connection

At the heart of tokenized stocks lies blockchain technology. To truly appreciate the significance of tokenized stocks, it is important to dive deeper into the essential role that blockchain technology plays in this financial revolution. Blockchain forms the backbone of tokenization, providing a secure, transparent, and immutable ledger for digital assets.

Reliability

At the core of blockchain’s contribution to tokenized stocks lies its immutability. Blockchain operates as a decentralized and distributed ledger where each transaction, once recorded, cannot be altered or deleted. This intrinsic feature ensures the integrity and transparency of ownership records, mitigating the risk of fraud and manipulation. Consequently, investors and businesses can trust that tokenized stock records are tamper-proof, fostering confidence in the market.

Security

Blockchain’s security protocols are renowned for their robustness. Blockchain networks safeguard digital assets against unauthorized access and cyber threats by employing cryptographic techniques and consensus mechanisms. This level of security is especially crucial in the context of tokenized stocks, where the protection of investors’ assets is paramount.

Speed

Traditional stock trading often involves lengthy settlement periods, leading to inefficiencies and counterparty risk. Blockchain technology eliminates these bottlenecks by enabling near-instantaneous settlement of tokenized stock transactions – especially with fast and cheap L2s such as Arbitrum or Optimism, almost instant Solana and Near, and dApp-chain of DyDx protocol running on Starkware. This not only enhances liquidity but also reduces operational costs, making tokenized stocks an attractive option for businesses and investors.

The blockchain connection in tokenized stocks is not merely a technical detail; it is the linchpin that enables the digitization, security, and global accessibility of traditional financial assets. It revolutionizes the way ownership and transactions are recorded, offering a new paradigm for businesses and investors to explore and leverage in an ever-evolving financial landscape.

Benefits of Tokenization for the Modern Business

Blockchain, the underlying technology of tokenized assets, is fast becoming the cornerstone of modern business processes.

The essential benefits of tokenization for nowadays businesses include:

  • Cost Efficiency: Tokenizing assets reduces the costs linked with managing and trading them. The streamlined process eliminates the need for intermediaries, thereby cutting down on excessive fees and commissions.
  • Global Reach and Liquidity: Tokenized stocks are accessible to global investors, providing businesses with an expanded audience. This global reach translates to enhanced liquidity, allowing businesses to tap into a larger capital pool.
  • Quick and Secure Transactions: With blockchain at its core, tokenized assets benefit from quicker and more secure transaction processes. This technology provides an immutable ledger, ensuring that every transaction is transparent and tamper-proof.
  • Fractional Ownership: One of the most transformative advantages of tokenization is the ability to divide assets into smaller, fractionalized parts. This enables businesses to raise capital by selling fractions of their assets, democratizing investment opportunities.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Modern platforms offering tokenization services incorporate regulatory tools, ensuring businesses remain compliant with local and international standards.

The Investor’s Perspective

From an investor’s viewpoint, tokenized shares are not merely an innovation but a pathway to a more democratized and efficient financial ecosystem. Let’s have a closer look at the key points of shares tokenization pros for investors:

  • Accessibility: Tokenized stocks break down barriers to entry, allowing investors from around the world to participate in global markets. This is especially valuable for individuals in regions with restricted access to certain markets.
  • Diversification: Knowing how to invest in tokenization, investors can diversify their portfolios like never before. Even with limited capital, one can invest in multiple assets, reducing risk and enhancing potential returns.
  • Transparent Transactions: Blockchain’s immutable ledger ensures that all transactions are transparent. Investors can track their investments and verify transactions without relying on third parties.
  • Real-Time Settlement: Unlike traditional stock exchanges which may take days for a transaction to settle, tokenized stocks offer near-instantaneous settlements, making the investment process smoother and faster.
  • Enhanced Security: Blockchain’s decentralized nature and cryptographic security ensure that investors’ assets and personal information are safe from hacks and unauthorized access.
