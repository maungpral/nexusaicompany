As a result, concerns regarding the long-term sustainability and future of NFT art proliferated. Despite these apprehensions, key industry players remain optimistic, actively contemplating and even anticipating a market rebirth.

HOW TO CREATE AN NFT PLATFORM IN 10 STEPS

1.Define the concept of your platform:

Clearly define the purpose, target audience, core functionalities of your platform and revenue-generating opportunities. Who are you trying to serve? What unique experience will you offer? Imagine your ideal user and tailor the platform experience to their specific needs and preferences.

2.Gather a reliable and experienced team:

Building a successful platform requires a skilled and experienced team. Hire developers with expertise in blockchain technology and NFT development, alongside talented designers, marketing professionals, and anyone else crucial to your vision. Prioritize individuals with a proven track record in relevant fields.

3.Take care of the technical development:

Choosing the right blockchain is paramount. Consider factors like users engagement, ecosystem, and liquidity. There’re several options which offer ready to work infrastructure solutions. Develop secure smart contracts to govern core functionalities and seamlessly integrate them with your chosen blockchain. Ensure your platform adheres to industry standards and best securitypractices.

4.Select and connect all the necessary infrastructure, including the chosen metadata storage provider:

Select and connect reliable 3rd-parties that seamlessly integrate with popular NFT marketplaces, wallets, and metadata storage providers. Prioritize reliability, security, and SDKs that align seamlessly with your platform’s overall architecture and functionalities.

5.Work on the UI/UX design:

Create an intuitive and user-friendly interface that makes navigating and interacting with your platform a joy. Prioritize visual appeal, accessibility, and a smooth user journey. Remember, first impressions matter – make sure your design is both beautiful and functional.

6.Complete the front-end part of the product development:

Translate your stunning UI/UX design into a functional and responsive front-end application. Optimize performance for speed and ensure cross-browser compatibility to reach a wider audience. Meticulously craft every pixel to deliver an exceptional user experience on any device.

7.Prioritize a comprehensive smart contract security audit before public release: 

Similar to testing of any final product, meticulously examining your platform’s smart contracts for vulnerabilities and potential exploits is crucial. Enlist qualified security professionals like Blaize to conduct this audit. By proactively addressing identified vulnerabilities, you significantly mitigate financial losses, reputational damage, and foster additional trust among your users.

8.Test the final product before public release:

Before unleashing your platform to the world, put it through rigorous testing. Conduct thorough functionality, security, and user experience tests across various devices and scenarios. Consider involving potential users in beta testing to gather valuable feedback and identify areas for improvement.

9.Deploy the platform and launch it in the market:

Deploy your platform on the chosen infrastructure and initiate a strategic marketing campaign. Build community engagement, attract early adopters, and generate media coverage to create buzz and excitement. Remember, a successful launch is just the beginning.

10.Provide continuous support and maintenance:

The whole process doesn’t end with launch. Continuously provide technical support, address user feedback, and implement platform updates to ensure its long-term success. Don’t forget to carry on security audits on a regular basis. Stay adaptable to evolving industry trends and user needs to keep your platform at the forefront of the NFT market.

Decide on The Type of Your NFT Platform

The first crucial step is selecting the platform that aligns with your vision. Remember, an NFT platform isn’t solely a marketplace – it’s your launchpad for diverse possibilities. Let’s explore various platform types to aid your informed decision:

1. Marketplaces: These familiar platforms facilitate buying, selling, and trading NFTs. They excel at connecting creators and collectors, providing exposure and liquidity. Ideal for established creators with ready-to-sell collections or projects focused on tradable assets.

2. Minting Platforms: Focus on creating and launching NFT collections. They offer tools for minting, setting smart contracts, and managing drops. Well-suited for artists, startups, or projects with unique collections needing tailored minting solutions.

3. Tokenization Platforms: These delve deeper, tokenizing real-world assets (e.g., real estate, artwork) into NFTs (think security token offerings or fractional ownership platforms). They bridge the gap between traditional and digital assets, unlocking new investment and ownership opportunities. Consider this if your project involves tokenizing tangible assets or creating unique ownership structures.

4. Gaming Platforms: Integrate NFTs into the gaming experience. These platforms enable in-game item ownership, character creation, and even play-to-earn mechanics. Perfect for projects aiming to build NFT-powered games or metaverses where users interact and derive value through NFTs.

We’ll describe the major types of NFT platforms below so that you can assess your needs and make a well-grounded decision.

NFT MARKETPLACE

The first option we’d like to mention is a universal trading platform for non-fungible assets. By creating your own NFT marketplace platform, you make NFTs a bit more available to a wider audience and, thus, speed up their global adoption.

Currently, there are a few popular websites for such purposes, so if you’d like to build an NFT marketplace platform, you’d better do thorough research before you kick into gear.

Here are just a few honorable mentions:

  • Blur – currently the biggest NFT marketplace, a rising star for professional traders, offering low fees, fast interface, and innovative features like fee-less bidding. Targets high-volume traders with advanced portfolio management tools.
  • OpenSea – is the world’s first web3 marketplace for NFTs and crypto collectibles. It enables creating and trading NFTs, starting auctions, and browsing digital art for inspiration.
  • Element Market – focuses on curated experiences and community. Offers a selective, high-quality collection of NFTs and fosters connections between collectors and creators through events and dedicated channels. Caters to collectors seeking unique pieces and a vibrant community.
  • Rarible – a multichain community-centric NFT marketplace that is known for low fees for NFT creators.

Most NFT marketplaces allow users to mint and trade different types of non-interchangeable tokens on Ethereum, BNB Chain, or other blockchains. In return for their services, marketplaces get a commission, which is the foundation of their revenue.

NFT COLLECTION

The digital art is still one of the most common NFT utilities no matter whether it’s the initial generation of monsters for a new game, the initial balance of shares from a tokenized villa, or even a preliminary art collection to raise money for further development of your platform.

The pros of NFT collections include the following:

  • Relatively simple development process as you won’t actually need to build an NFT platform – you can easily mint your NFTs on one of the existing marketplaces.
  • The possibility to receive quick profit if you have a strong community or market your collection well.
  • Plenty of choices when it comes to NFT utility – your collection can consist of digital art, music, AR/VR items, real-world objects, metaverse real estate, and much more.

Even though NFT collections don’t require separate platforms, you can still create one for your company. Yet, in this case, the whole NFT platform development would take much more time and resources.

NFT IN DEFI

Decentralized finance also provides a vast field for NFT application. However, NFT utility here is much less evident than when it comes to gaming or marketplaces, so let us guide you through it.

First, NFTs might serve as liquidity provider (LP) position representation, similar to the Uniswap V3 solution. Based on the pool and the selected parameters, users will receive a unique NFT representing their position in that specific pool.

Second, there is a separate niche of NFT lending that allows for collateralizing users’ NFTs as a loan in exchange for immediate crypto payment.

Third, we have various financial instruments inherited from traditional finance and transformed into DeFi NFT-based solutions. These are NFT bonds, options, and different derivatives in the form of NFTs.

NFTs can have many other DeFi utilities, like serving as a yield farming boost, so we’ll leave the rest to your imagination and business needs.

NFT AS A KEY TO SMART ACCOUNTS

Beyond their current application as static assets, NFTs are poised to unlock a new dimension of user interaction – serving as the keys to smart accounts enabled by the ERC-6551 standard. This transformative integration elevates NFTs from ownership tokens into personalized passports to a secure, on-chain identity. 

Imagine an NFT not simply representing digital ownership, but acting as the exclusive key to a smart contract wallet – your gateway to interacting with DeFi protocols, managing digital assets, and executing complex financial transactions, all without the traditional vulnerabilities of custodial wallets.

NFT PLATFORM COMPONENTS

NFT platforms exhibit diverse specializations, including marketplaces, tokenization platforms, RWA protocols, simple minting pages, and sophisticated digital art auctions. Despite their distinct functionalities, these platforms share many common components and necessitate a comprehensive development approach involving a team of professionals.

Smart Contracts at the Core: Smart contracts are fundamental in decentralizing protocols, with NFTs being a primary application. Platforms typically require standards such as ERC721, ERC1155, or newer ones like ERC-5956 and ERC-6220. Their implementations are often straightforward, thanks to libraries like OpenZeppelin and ERC721A, which provide fully audited contracts with essential plugins. While other chains have unique NFT standards, most adhere to traditional EVM standards, facilitating development across various platforms including Solana and Sui.
However, there are a few points that require additional attention:

  • Royalty: This may involve ERC-2981 or other formats agreed upon by the marketplace and demands high attention from the security team.
  • Minting Process: Airdrops, batch mints, auctions, and sales usually incorporate custom logic, necessitating an experienced team to adapt proven concepts to the protocol’s needs.

Metadata and Storage: A crucial element of any NFT platform is the metadata associated with NFTs, including the infrastructure for its storage, which involves SDKs and gateways. 

For storage, there are many reliable solutions like Moralis, Alchemy, Arweave, and Filecoin, offering both storage and tools for efficient metadata management. Integration into the project’s pipeline is essential. While working with pure IPFS might not be necessary, automating metadata formatting, optimizing the NFT minting process, and connecting storage SDK endpoints with your dApp and backend services are crucial. 

Blaize manages all integration processes and additional development for rapid metadata management for thousands of NFTs, handling metadata updates for flippers and metaverse projects, or uploading digital certificates for RWA protocols.

dApp and UI: The purpose of your NFT platform might vary, but essential components include:

  • Explorer Functionality: To view assets or browse existing collections.
  • Basic Transfer, Minting, and Sale Interfaces: Allowing users to interact with NFTs on your platform.
  • Integrated Wallets: Such as MetaMask, WalletConnect, Coinbase wallet, or specific chain wallets like Phantom and Solflare for Solana, or Martian and SuiWallet for Sui.

Frontend and web3 engineers are required to build the dApp with all necessary functionality. Additionally, UI/UX design, covered by our in-house designers, is crucial for an engaging user experience.

Offchain Services: Despite their decentralized nature, every dApp requires backend services. NFT platforms are no exception, necessitating services for aggregating and indexing on-chain information, bootstrapping and caching metadata, providing reliable price feeds, and maintaining databases for prices, transaction histories, and user accounts. Even a small NFT minting page will need integration with tools like Subgraph, Airstack, or Moralis SDK, enabling functionalities like displaying all NFT owners and sales history. A backend engineer is thus an indispensable part of the team.

Even More Smart Contracts: Depending on the platform type, additional functionality often requires the development of more smart contracts, such as:

  • Upgrades and Wraps for NFTs
  • Various Auctions, Drops, and Sale Types
  • NFT Locks, Staking, Depositing as Collateral
  • Tokenization Pipelines and Vaults for NFT Fractioning
  • Custom Solutions or Protocol Integrations as Needed by the Platform

NFT MARKETPLACE FEATURES FOR DIFFERENT PARTIES

Let’s say you decided to create an NFT platform for trading assets. What should you start with, and which functionality should you include?

If you’re wondering how to develop your own NFT marketplace, one of the most important things to consider is business logic. The underlying smart contracts must work impeccably so that none of the involved parties could get deceived or feel uncomfortable while using your product.

We’ll describe the key functionality for different categories of users, including buyers, sellers, and administrators. Make sure to develop a solution that considers all of these points, and don’t hesitate to add custom features that would be unique to your project.

So, the parties of this process include:

1.NFT Buyer:

  • Registration and authorization.
  • Connecting the wallet.
  • Browsing NFTs, searching with keywords or tags.
  • Completing the purchase, whether through a fixed payment or an auction.
  • Completing the transaction, including gas fees and a platform commission.

2.NFT Seller:

  • Registration and authorization.
  • A dedicated space for creating NFTs.
  • The possibility to transfer NFTs from other marketplaces.
  • Choosing a place to store NFTs before minting them.
  • The possibility to select a custom name, description, store images and other files when creating an NFT.
  • Creating collections.
  • Selecting the type of payment – fixed or auctioned. Creating auctions.
  • Putting the NFT for sale and ending the sale if their intentions have changed.
  • Revising the price of an NFT.
  • Receiving the funds for the sold NFTs.

3.Administrator:

  • Viewing all NFTs created on the platform.
  • Viewing all users and their trading history.
  • The possibility to remove NFTs in case there are duplicated assets or ones that break the Terms of Use of the platform.
  • Editing and adding categories of assets.
  • Adding users to the whitelist if necessary and blocking them.
  • Managing fees for buyers and sellers.
  • Setting up the integration with IPFS for file storage.
  • Other necessary technical functionality peculiar to your platform.

Regardless of the chosen NFT platform features and its type, you’ll need to use APIs and third-party services to extend the functionality. Most of these solutions will be connected with data storage, which is crucial for the NFT industry.

The vital part is choosing a secure service and integrating it into your platform correctly. That is why hiring NFT developers with proven expertise should be your number one priority.
