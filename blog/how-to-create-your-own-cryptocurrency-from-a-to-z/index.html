What Is The Difference Between Crypto Coins And Tokens?

If you are just starting your journey in the crypto industry and want to figure out how to create your own digital currency, there are quite a few questions you might be asking. But one of the basic things to cover first is the difference between two notions: coins and tokens.

Simply put, a coin is a native blockchain-specific asset that is distributed among miners or validators. A token, on the other hand, is a secondary notion that is secured by smart contracts and has a specific utility or security function.

We have discussed this difference in more detail and provided some examples in one of our previous articles, so make sure to check it out to learn more.

Ways to Create a Cryptocurrency

Depending on the type of asset you‚Äôve set your mind on in the previous step, there might be different ways to create a cryptocurrency. All of them have their pros and cons, which we‚Äôll describe below.

Create a New Blockchain

As we have mentioned before, developing a new blockchain to create your own crypto coin is one of the most difficult, expensive, and time-consuming solutions. This will require you to hire an experienced team of blockchain developers with specific industry knowledge and spend a lot on the tech stack.

Another issue with this method is the necessity to attract an audience to a newly created blockchain, which might be a major challenge in the current market.

And yet, new blockchain creation comes with certain benefits that experienced developers can appreciate. For instance, you can implement new principles or a whole new ecosystem on the chain.

Fork an Existing Blockchain

If you are sure that your business needs a coin, but you have a limited budget and don‚Äôt want to hire too many specialists, this is a solution for you. 

Instead of creating your own blockchain, consider forking an existing one. This way, you will be able to cut costs and save time since all that your team will have to do is alter the source code of an existing network and launch your new coin.

However, be aware that forking an existing blockchain may lead to potential security issues or increased market volatility as traders adjust to the creation of the new cryptocurrency.

Use an Existing Cryptocurrency Creation Platform

This is by far the easiest way to create a cryptocurrency token and the most cost-efficient one. Using an existing platform with a ready-made set of developer tools will allow your team to develop and launch a token fast and with no extra headache. And still, you would need to hire knowledgeable developers with experience in working with smart contracts to build a secure solution.

Best Solutions for Creating Your Own Crypto Tokens & Coins

Since creating a token on the existing platform is one of the easiest and most cost-efficient solutions, let‚Äôs talk about it in detail.

Currently, there are a few blockchains and ecosystems that allow for developing new tokens and even provide suitable tools for that. The most popular and stable ones are EVMs and NEAR.

As a token development company, our team has expertise in creating tokens on popular platforms like EVMs and NEAR. We can guide you through the entire process and provide the necessary tools for a seamless token creation experience.

1. ERC20

Ethereum was the first chain to offer custom token creation. Later, many EVM chains were developed on its basis, which also allowed developers to deploy their own ERC20 tokens. Such chains include Binance Smart Chain, Avalanche, Tron, Fantom, and many more.

ERC20 tokens are the most popular in the current market and bring a few benefits:

  • Easy and fast development
  • Cost-effectiveness
  • Easy usage and adoption by a wider audience
  • Quick fundraising
  • Versatility

Programming language: Solidity.

2. NEP20

Another popular option is deploying a NEP20 token in the NEAR ecosystem. Even though this chain is not as old as Ethereum, it has gained an impressive reputation over the years and attracted a huge community of supporters.

Programming language: Rust.

On top of the token creation through different platforms, you can create your own coin in a few ways that are a bit easier than others.

3. Fork Bitcoin

One of the easiest ways to develop a crypto coin is by forking. You can fork any chain from the Bitcoin family, including Bitcoin itself, Dogecoin, Litecoin, Dash, and so on. However, as we have mentioned before, it would still require you a lot more time and funding than making a cryptocurrency on existing platforms.

4. Substrate chain or Polkadot parachain

One more popular but rather long way to create a cryptocurrency token is in the Polkadot network. The ecosystem offers a unique concept of parachains, application-specific chains that enable building whatever protocol you want with almost no limitations.
